What do we analyze?

Staying compliant with evolving regulatory standards is crucial in the cryptocurrency world. Regular checks help you adhere to legal requirements, avoiding penalties and legal issues.

High Risk

  • Child Exploitation

    Wallets and entities linked to child exploitation materials or activities. Immediate reporting and avoidance required.

  • Dark Market

    Cryptocurrency traced to darknet marketplaces selling illegal goods and services. High risk of legal consequences.

  • Dark Service

    Funds connected to serious criminal activities including child abuse, terrorist financing, or international drug trafficking operations.

  • Enforcement Action

    Addresses under active investigation or legal proceedings by law enforcement agencies. Transactions may be frozen or seized.

  • Fraudulent Exchange

    Exchanges involved in exit scams, illegal operations, or whose assets have been confiscated by authorities.

  • Mixer & Tumbler

    Funds processed through mixing services to obscure transaction history. Primarily used for money laundering and hiding illicit origins.

  • Scam & Stolen Coins

    Cryptocurrency obtained through fraud, phishing, hacks, or theft. May result in account freezes and legal complications.

  • Sanctions

    Wallets and entities on OFAC, UN, or EU sanctions lists. Transactions are illegal and subject to severe penalties.

Medium Risk

  • Unverified Exchanges

    Trading platforms operating without proper licensing or regulatory oversight. May lack adequate security and compliance measures.

  • Privacy Coins

    Cryptocurrencies like Monero or Zcash with built-in anonymity features. Often flagged by exchanges due to regulatory concerns.

  • High-Risk Jurisdictions

    Transactions originating from countries with weak AML regulations or limited international cooperation on financial crime.

  • Gambling Services

    Unlicensed online casinos and betting platforms that may not comply with gambling regulations or anti-money laundering standards.

  • P2P Exchanges

    Peer-to-peer platforms with minimal or no KYC requirements. Increased risk of encountering illicit funds or unverified counterparties.

Trusted Sources

  • Regulated Exchanges

    Fully licensed cryptocurrency exchanges operating under strict regulatory compliance with robust KYC/AML procedures and user protection.

  • Verified Institutions

    Traditional banks, payment processors, and financial institutions with established regulatory oversight and compliance frameworks.

  • Known Entities

    Recognized businesses, merchants, and service providers with proven track records and transparent operations in the crypto space.

  • DeFi Protocols

    Decentralized finance platforms that have undergone comprehensive security audits and maintain transparent, reputable operations.

  • Verified Wallets

    Wallet addresses that have completed KYC verification processes and are linked to identified, legitimate users or entities.
